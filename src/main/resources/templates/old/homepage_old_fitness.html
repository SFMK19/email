Frequently Asked Questions

For new bodybuilders, it's recommended to start with general basic strength training and cardio. This can help you build muscle strength and improve cardiorespulmonary endurance. Consider joining beginner yoga, a relaxing group fitness class, or using low-impact aerobic machinery such as a treadmill or elliptical. It is recommended to gradually increase the intensity and complexity of training under the guidance of a professional coach to ensure safe and effective achievement of fitness goals.

Three to five training sessions per week, each lasting 30 to 60 minutes, are generally recommended, with the frequency and duration adjusted according to an individual's health status, fitness goals and schedule. It is important to give the body enough time to recover, especially after high intensity or strength intensive training. Consistent, regular exercise leads to more long-term health benefits than intermittent and casual exercise.

The key to avoiding injury is to take the right precautions, including proper warming up and cooling down, using the right posture and technique, and listening to your body's signals. Warming up increases muscle temperature and flexibility, reducing the risk of injury; Correct exercise posture and techniques can ensure that all parts of the body are correctly stressed during exercise; Proper rest and recovery are equally important. If you have any doubts or discomfort, consult a professional fitness instructor or medical professional.
